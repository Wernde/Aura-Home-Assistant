# Light-first household command centre

## Decision

The primary AURA wall display uses a light-first, neutral command-centre layout. Dark appearance is optional for low-light use and is not the default identity.

## Layout model

- Left: verified household context and what matters next.
- Centre: AURA, conversation, current state and up to three contextual facts.
- Right: household actions, scenes, family tools and one useful insight.
- Bottom: whole-system health only.

## Information hierarchy

1. Immediate: AURA state, urgent alerts, current conversation and active action.
2. Important today: next event, weather change, security exception and active scene.
3. Available tools: shopping, detailed controls, settings and energy detail.

## Density rules

- Ambient Home must answer “Is everything all right?” and “What matters next?” without requiring navigation.
- Default Home content is reduced by roughly one third from the previous dashboard.
- No more than three contextual facts remain permanently around AURA.
- Detailed energy, connection and perception controls remain available but visually secondary.
- Repeated nested borders are avoided; spacing and grouping provide hierarchy.

## Visual rules

- Warm white, pale stone and mist grey carry normal surfaces.
- Blue indicates active intelligence, selection, links and primary action.
- Green means healthy, secure or confirmed.
- Amber means attention, pending or upcoming change.
- Red means urgent, failed or hazardous.
- Purple is reserved for scenes, atmosphere and personalisation.
- Colour is always paired with a label, icon or shape.

## Wall-display readability

- The main greeting is the largest text after the clock.
- Primary card labels remain readable from approximately two to five metres.
- Touch targets remain at least 44 px and are generally 48–56 px.
- Secondary text is reduced or hidden before primary information is compressed.

## Interaction modes

### Ambient

Low density, one useful insight, clear home health and quiet voice invitation.

### Engaged

AURA gains visual emphasis, the voice dock activates and contextual facts become interactive.

### Focused workspace

One task dominates while AURA remains visible. Supporting information is progressively disclosed.

### Alert

Normal information recedes. The verified alert, source, severity and primary action become dominant.

## Safety

The redesign does not change the confirmed Home Assistant command lifecycle. Real-device actions remain allowlisted and user-facing success still requires observed target state.
